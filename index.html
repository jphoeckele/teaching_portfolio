My Statement Of Teaching Philosophy

Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet consectetur.

My teaching values are based on a belief that learning needs to be student-centered and that students should be equal partners in the learning process. I work with students whose age, cognitive development, cultural, and language backgrounds differ greatly. While teaching I noticed that often students who share the same culture can still have opposite personalities that affect their learning abilities. Understanding the nature of the relationship between language and culture, is central in the process of learning another language. Taking all these aspects into consideration, I understand that what is good for one student may not be appropriate for another, because each person is unique. This makes the teaching profession more interesting since being a teacher means that I should never stop learning and improving myself both as a student and an instructor.
Using different techniques and interesting assignments I manage to create a motivating, friendly, and encouraging environment where students try to do their best in learning. I understand that second language acquisition can be affected by personality factors, such as self-concept, risk-taking, anxiety, empathy, and extroversion, etc. However, I am aware that sometimes students should be able to accept possible “failure” in their attempts. Then, they assess all the facets of that failure and turn it into an experience that teaches them something about how to tailor the process so that they may still achieve the desired results.
I strongly value the diversity of learning styles and the unique perspectives - both individual and cultural - that my students may bring into the classroom. As a result, I strive to provide an environment where students feel comfortable in expressing their needs and opinions and I believe that the entire class benefits and learns from this process.During one semester, I had the opportunity to teach both En100 Composition classes and in the Intensive English program. This helped me realize how much the methodology and basic principles of teaching can slightly vary when considering the audience. This experience taught me that in all situations it is necessary for a teacher to communicate effectively. I also believe that praising students for their attempts to succeed rather than criticizing their failures creates a friendly and challenging environment that is better suited for the learning process.
I also devote special attention to the error correction process. Addressing an error incorrectly can often lead to more confusion. That’s why I often take notes and plan an activity for a later lesson. In my opinion, each assignment and lesson should be evaluated according to the expectations and goals of the specific course. Thus, the learning process should start with easier tasks and gradually become more complicated as the students’ understanding grows. By modeling good communication and a real enthusiasm for the subject matter, I attempt to instill in every student the desire to learn not only the material but also to embrace the culture that created it.
